Services

TMI's services can make an immediate difference to how you experience your information and your digital devices, but that's not the only goal. Digital files are highly impermanent, and the information they contain often needs to last much longer than a digital device. You may have the experience of doing some digital spring cleaning, enjoying the benefits for a few months, before falling behind again.

The goal of TMI's services to address your information needs in the present and to set you up to meet those needs for the next ten years. No scheme is perfect, but TMI's services will allow you to manage your information efficiently and incrementally. No more spring cleaning or lengthy migrations, no more painful deletions or outdated versions. Just personal information, preserved, efficient, and sensible.

Systems

Files

Make the most of your filesystem. Save space and organize your files to facilitate preservation, productivity, and peace of mind. TMI works with all major OS and cloud file systems.

Notes

Learn to leverage notes. Use note taking systems and applications to more easily manage information, launch projects, and meet personal goals. TMI will work with note systems you already use or are provided by your workplace, or will recommend and set up a note taking system.

Calendar

Maximize your calendar. Learn to use your calendar to protect and manage your time and productivity. TMI will work with you to identify how your calendar fits into your current work and personal goals.

Tasks

Get to-dos done. Complete everything from tasks to complex projects using the to-do system provided to you by your workplace or recommended by TMI.

Email and Messaging

Master your inbox. Get more, faster from email and messaging and minimize unwanted distractions. TMI works with all major email and messaging providers, including Gmail, Outlook, Slack, and Teams.

Strategies

TMI's advanced services go beyond systems to show you how to make your information work for you.

FKR (Find, Keep, Retrieve)

Develop the skills and strategies to more easily find, keep, and retrieve important information. TMI will show you how systems can work in concert to facilitate FKR when information is old, complex, or fragmented across repositories. FKR will become a bedrock for mitigating information overload and reclaiming time.

Sense-making

Learn to turn information into action. Sense-making skills and strategies will show you how you can use information to stay apprised of deadlines, develop and complete complex projects, and make consistent progress towards your personal and professional goals.

Preservation

Preserve your important professional or personal information for the future using digital preservation techniques employed by libraries and museums.

Take the Diagnostic

All TMI sessions begin with a diagnostic and service recommendation. TMI uses two diagnostic techniques: critical incident and "the timer."

When you contact TMI, you will be asked to do a critical incident diagnostic.

Critical Incident

The critical incident diagnostic asks you to reflect on an incident where your information strategies resulted in a meaningful failure. The failure may be extreme, or it may have been a significant waste of time. Any unpleasant event caused by a failure of your information to work for you can be considered a critical incident. In the case where you're concerned a pattern of events, try to think of a specific time the pattern occurred.

About Jack

TMI Services is the project of Jack OMalley. Jack is a librarian and archivist with experience working at some of the premier information organizations in the United States. He is a graduate of Yale University and the Pratt Institute. The mission of TMI is to bring professional information science strategies to the personal information collections of anyone grappling with digital information overload.
